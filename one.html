Introduction

Starting the Gym might seem overwhelming; therefore, I wanted to create a guide to help you through the journey. There are many benefits gained from going to the Gym, such as; improving your overall health, strength, stability, muscle coordination, and cognitive health (improved memory, concentration, and mental sharpness). This guide aims to be a foundation for you to build upon. You need to be conscious of a defined end goal (The person you want to be at the end of the transformation). Due to the society that we live in, people are becoming increasingly inactive. Therefore, we need to take time out of our day to get the necessary amount of exercise. The body is excellent at adapting; if you apply resistance, it will grow. This applies to most areas of people’s lives. I think a lot of people underestimate the capabilities of their bodies. Invest in yourself. Having a clear and objective goal is essential. What is the result you are looking for? Is it losing Weight? Gain muscle, and in what areas? These are important to think about to achieve the best outcome from the Gym. The most IMPORTANT thing is to be consistent; 3 to 4 times a week in the Gym, with an improved diet, is more than enough to achieve your goals. Having a journal can help you keep track during your fitness journey. You can write the number of sets, workouts, and other daily tasks. Here are some great journals that I use:

Calories

Tracking your calories can be daunting, but the benefit is enormous. It starts by understanding the number of calories you intake and how often you are active. The more active you are, the higher the calories are needed; vice versa, the less active you are, the fewer calories are required. If you are trying to lose weight, try a deficit of 300cal in your first month and then move to a 500cal deficit. Doing an extreme deficit could lead to an effect on your metabolism. The body will start to hold on to fat as a means of a panic response. Therefore, you need to see what deficit works best for your body. Here are three things that can help you understand and track your calories:

  1. TDEE Calculator - Use the TDEE calculator to learn your Total Daily Energy Expenditure, a measure of how many calories you burn per day. This calorie calculator will also display your BMI, BMR, Macros & many other useful statistics!
  2. TDEE Spreadsheet (DOWNLOAD!!) This can help you attain your desired body weight by calculating the calories you need to be in a deficit per day. Plug in your weight and calories on the sheet every day.
  3. App – MyFitnessPal

Legs

  • Squats: Begin by standing with your feet about shoulder-width apart and your toes pointed slightly outward. Slowly lower your body by bending your knees until your thighs are parallel to the floor. Hold for one second and then press through your heels to return to the starting position.
  • Lunges: Start by standing with feet shoulder-width apart. Take a large step forward with your right leg, followed by your left leg, so your feet are in a staggered stance. Bend both knees until your right thigh is parallel to the floor and your left knee hovers just above it. Push back up to the starting position and repeat with your left leg.
  • Step-ups: Step onto a box or bench with your right foot. Push up through your heel, lifting your left foot onto the box. Step back down with your left foot followed by your right. Repeat the same on the other side.
  • Leg Extensions: Adjust the seat of the leg extension machine so that your legs are aligned with the axis of the machine. Grasp the handles and extend your legs until they are straight. Slowly lower your legs back to the starting position.
  • Leg Curls: Adjust the seat of the leg curl machine so that your knees are aligned with the axis of the machine. Grasp the handles and curl your legs up as far as you can. Slowly lower your legs back to the starting position.
  • Calf Raises: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold onto a bench or wall for support. Rise up onto the balls of your feet and hold for one second. Lower your heels back to the ground and repeat.

Chest

  • Barbell Bench Press: Start by lying flat on the bench with your feet firmly planted on the floor. Position the barbell above your chest with your arms fully extended. Lower the barbell to just below nipple level, pause, and then push the bar back up to its starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Incline Dumbbell Press: Sit on an inclined bench with the backrest at a 45-degree angle. Hold two dumbbells above your chest with your arms fully extended. Lower the dumbbells to just below nipple level, pause, and then push the weights back up to their starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Cable Flyes: Attach two handles to the low pulley on a cable machine. Stand facing away from the machine with your arms extended. Start with your arms slightly bent and palms facing each other. Pull the handles together in front of your chest and pause. Slowly return to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Chest Dips: Place your hands on the edge of a dip bar with your arms extended. Lower your body until your elbows are at a 90-degree angle, pause, and then press

Back

  • Deadlift: Start with a barbell deadlift for 8-12 repetitions. Adjust the weight to a comfortable level.
  • Seated Rows: Perform seated rows for 8-12 repetitions.
  • Lat Pulldowns: Do lat pulldowns for 8-12 repetitions.
  • Reverse Flys: Do reverse flys for 8-12 repetitions.
  • Bent Over Rows: Perform bent over rows for 8-12 repetitions.

Shoulder

  • Seated Dumbbell Shoulder Press: Sit on a bench with a dumbbell in each hand and your feet flat on the floor. Make sure your back is straight and your core is engaged.
  • Lateral Raises: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and a dumbbell in each hand. Keep your arms at your sides and your palms facing inwards. Raise your arms out to the sides, keeping the dumbbells level with your shoulders. Lower your arms back down to the starting position.
  • Rear Deltoid Flyes: Stand or sit with a slight forward lean, holding a pair of dumbbells. With a slight bend in your elbows, raise the weights out to the sides until your arms are parallel to the floor. Slowly lower them back down and repeat for 8-12 reps.

Triceps

  • Triceps Pushdowns: Using a cable machine, attach a straight bar to the cable. Stand with your feet hip-width apart and hold the bar with both hands at chest height. With your elbows tight to your body, press the bar downwards and then slowly lift back up to the starting position. Complete 3 sets of 8-12 reps.

Bicep

  • Barbell Curls: Start by standing up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart. Grasp a barbell with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Curl the barbell up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body. Pause at the top and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Dumbbell Curls: Start by standing up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart. Grasp a dumbbell in each hand with an overhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Curl each dumbbell up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body. Pause at the top and slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Cable Curls: Stand in the middle of a cable crossover machine with a handle in each hand. Your arms should be extended and your palms facing up. Curl the handles up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body. Pause at the top and slowly lower the handles back to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Hammer Curls: Start by standing up straight with your feet shoulder-width apart. Grasp a dumbbell in each hand with a neutral grip (palms facing in). Curl the dumbbells up towards your shoulders, keeping your elbows close to your body. Pause at the top and slowly lower the dumbbells back to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.
  • Preacher Curls: Sit on a preacher curl bench and grasp a barbell with an underhand grip, hands shoulder-width apart. Rest your elbows on the bench and curl the barbell up towards your shoulders. Pause at the top and slowly lower the barbell back to the starting position. Repeat for the desired number of reps.

Forearms

  • Barbell Wrist Curls: Sit on a bench and hold a barbell in both hands with an underhand grip, your palms facing up. Allow the barbell to hang in your hands and curl your wrists up, bringing the weight as close to your forearms as possible. Lower the barbell back down and repeat for 8-12 reps.
  • Reverse Barbell Wrist Curls: Sit on a bench and hold a barbell in both hands with an overhand grip, your palms facing down. Allow the barbell to hang in your hands and curl your wrists up, bringing the weight as close to your forearms as possible. Lower the barbell back down and repeat for 8-12 reps.
  • Hammer Curls: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a pair of dumbbells in each hand with a neutral grip, your palms facing each other. Curl the weights up towards your shoulders and then lower them back down. Repeat for 8-12 reps.

Abs

  • Standing Cable Crunches: Start by standing in front of a cable machine with a rope attachment. Hold the rope with both hands, keeping your arms straight and close to your ears. Without moving your legs, use your abs to crunch your torso down towards your hips. Slowly return to the starting position. Repeat for 10-12 reps.
  • Reverse Crunches: Begin by lying flat on the floor with your hands by your sides and your knees bent. Keeping your knees together, use your abs to lift your hips off the floor and bring your knees up to your chest. Slowly lower your legs and hips back down to the floor. Repeat for 10-12 reps.
  • . Medicine Ball Twists: Begin by standing with your feet hip-width apart and holding a medicine ball in front of you with both hands. Twist your torso to the left and then to the right, making sure to keep your arms straight throughout the motion. Repeat for 10-12 reps.

Obliques

  • Russian Twists: Sit on the floor with your knees bent and feet flat. Lean back slightly so that your torso is at a 45 degree angle and your arms are extended in front of you. Twist your torso to the right and then to the left, engaging your obliques with each twist. Do 10-15 reps.
  • Side Bends: Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a dumbbell in your left hand. Bend to the side, engaging your obliques, and then return to the starting position. Do 10-15 reps then switch sides.
  • Bicycle Crunches: Lie on your back with your hands behind your head and your knees bent. Lift your right shoulder towards your left knee and then switch sides. Do 10-15 reps.

Full Body Workout

A full-body workout consists of compound movement, which requires you to use more than one type of muscle group. This type of exercise is great when you first get started in the Gym. Nice to do it once a week.

To do a full-body workout do this:

  • Deadlift
  • Squat
  • Bench press or Incline bench press
  • Barbell row
  • Shoulder Press
